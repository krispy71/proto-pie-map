2. What data we collect and why

2a. Server access logs

When you visit this site, our server automatically records a standard access log entry. This is a routine security measure that allows us to detect abuse, diagnose errors, and monitor application health.

Each log entry contains:

  • Your anonymised IP address — the last octet of IPv4 addresses and the last 64 bits of IPv6 addresses are replaced with zeros before the entry is written. For example, 192.168.1.42 becomes 192.168.1.0.
  • The date and time of your request
  • The page or resource you requested
  • Your browser's User-Agent string (browser type and version)
  • The HTTP status code returned (e.g. 200, 404)

We do not log full, unanonymised IP addresses. We do not link log entries to individual users or build behavioural profiles.

Legal basis (GDPR Art. 6(1)(f)): Legitimate interest — security monitoring and abuse prevention are necessary for operating a publicly accessible web service.

Retention: Logs are stored in AWS CloudWatch with a 30-day retention policy and are automatically deleted thereafter.

2b. Third-party map tile providers

The map is displayed using tile images loaded from third-party content delivery networks. When your browser loads a tile image, it sends a connection request (including your IP address and browser headers) to the tile provider's servers. We do not control what data these providers collect.

The providers used, and their privacy policies:

  • CARTO (CartoDB) — Light, Dark, and Voyager tile styles.
    Privacy policy: carto.com/privacy
  • OpenTopoMap — Topographic tile style. Operated by volunteers; data from OpenStreetMap.
    See opentopomap.org/about
  • Esri / ArcGIS Online — ESRI Relief tile style.
    Privacy policy: esri.com/privacy

Tile providers are contacted only when you interact with the map (pan, zoom, or change the map style). The Light (Positron) tile style loads by default; others are loaded only if you select them.

2c. Leaflet.js library

The interactive map engine (Leaflet.js version 1.9.4) is served directly from this site. No connection to an external CDN is made to load it. Your browser does not contact any third-party server to load the map engine.

3. Cookies and tracking

This site sets no cookies of any kind. There are no analytics scripts, advertising networks, social media buttons, or tracking pixels. Your browser may cache static files (images, scripts, stylesheets) for up to 24 hours as a standard performance optimisation; this cache is stored locally on your device and is not accessible to us.

4. Your rights under GDPR

If you are in the European Economic Area (EEA) or United Kingdom, you have rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and equivalent UK law, including:

  • Right to access — you may request a copy of any personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to erasure — you may request deletion of your data. Because we anonymise IPs before logging, we cannot identify which log entries, if any, relate to you.
  • Right to object — you may object to processing based on legitimate interest.

To exercise these rights, contact us at the address in section 1. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority (for EU residents: your member state's supervisory authority; for UK residents: the ICO).

If we make material changes to this policy, we will update the date at the top of this page. Continued use of the site after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
